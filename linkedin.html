optimize communication with prospective talent

Let's make it simpler and lighter

LinkedIn is the largest profesional social networking platform in the world. LinkedIn Recruiter is a subscription-based product that Linkedin offers to give recruiters to ability to source from within LinkedIn's talent network.

For this project, I conducted a brief study with recruiters who use LinkedIn Recruiter's iOS app to see what they liked, disliked, and wished the app could do. What I discovered was that LinkedIn's Recruiter app was far more robust than it needed to be, which inspired me to design a lighter version of the app focusing on the core needs of its users: Recruiters.

Current Design: Long Story, Short

Quickly into my casual survey of Recruiter iOS users, one thing was very clear: They ONLY use the app for it's messeging. For Recruiter users, their messages go through a different channel than the actual LinkedIn, so message threads with candidates are only visible in the Recruiter application. To make this project short and sweet, I scrapped all other aspects of Recruiter iOS, and focused solely on it's messaging experience.

It doesn't take long to notice that LinkedIn Recruiter iOS is an outdated app. The screen-caps below only include the messeging experience since it was clear the rest of the app could be done without. One of the most notable callouts from users was that Recruiter iOS bears no resemblence to the LinkedIn iOS app. The second most popular callout was how messy the inbox was. Upon further observation, I found that Recruiter iOS doesn't have message threads, and every message comes through independently. Additionally, you're unable to see your most recent sent message when communicating with candidates, which makes it confusing to know where you left off the conversation.

Designing a New Experience

The main visual inspiration for my redesign was LinkedIn's flagship iOS app. The first thing I focused on was brightening the application and introducing a color palette more aligned with LinkedIn's current design. Another notable change is the thread-based messaging experience. No more one-off messeges. Now you can view your entire conversation and know exactly where you left off.

Other Subtle Changes

Outside of the messaging experience, there wasn't much to include, and was mostly an update of the settings and preferences (no longer a separate page, but a slide-out feature). The only major thing I did add to the app, was a more distinct 'Notifications' option in the settings, since the current app had no notification options, which is a whole different user pain point in itself. Final Summary

After surveying users and diving into a redesign of an app like Recruiter iOS, it was interesting to me how my redesign wasn't much of a redesign as it was an entirely new application. Because of this, I decided this app needed to separate itself from the "Recruiter" identity since, technically, it doesn't do much of what Recruiter is capable of doing. Thus, I came up with the name "InMail Recruiter" to indicate that the primary use of this app is to interact with your InMail and nothing more. Super simple, and super light.
